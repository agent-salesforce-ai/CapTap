Common Questions

Everything you need to know about CapTap merchant cash advances. If we don't answer it here, email ops@captap.co.

An MCA is an advance on your future sales. You receive a lump sum and repay through a small percentage of your daily credit card or bank deposits. It's not a loan — it's a purchase of your future receivables, which means more flexible qualification.
We don't have a minimum credit score requirement. We focus primarily on your business revenue and cash flow. Even business owners with credit challenges can qualify if they have strong, consistent deposits.
Most applications are reviewed within 3 hours during business days. Once approved and you sign the agreement, funds are typically wired to your account within 24 hours — often same day.
Just 3 months of business bank statements and a valid ID. That's it. No tax returns, no business plans, no financial projections required.
Checking your eligibility involves only a soft credit inquiry, which does not impact your score. A hard inquiry only occurs if you accept an offer and proceed with funding.
We typically advance 50% – 100% of your average monthly revenue as a single draw. Minimum $10K, maximum $2M. Your underwriter will propose a tiered structure so you can pick a size and term that fits your cash flow.
MCAs are priced as a factor rate, not an APR. On a $100K advance at 1.28, you repay $128K total over the term. Rates range from 1.18 (prime) to 1.49 (second-look). There are no prepayment penalties — paying early reduces your effective cost.
Most deals use a fixed daily ACH debit from your business bank account (Monday – Friday). Larger advances and tier-A deals can be structured as weekly. The debit amount is set on day one and doesn't change — no surprises.
Yes, we write 2nd and 3rd-position deals when the math works. Send us your current advances' balances and daily debits and we'll tell you within 3 hours whether a stacked offer makes sense.
We fund merchants in all 50 U.S. states. State-by-state disclosures are provided in your offer packet. MCAs are purchase-of-receivables agreements, not loans, so they're regulated differently from state to state — ask your rep if you want specifics.
